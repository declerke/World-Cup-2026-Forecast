# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Football fans and data enthusiasts who want to understand World Cup 2026 probabilities, not just watch match results. They're curious, quantitatively comfortable enough to read a probability bar, and using this on a laptop or desktop the evening before or during a match — engaged, expectant, looking for signal.

## Product Purpose

CupCast 2026 is a live ML forecast for the FIFA World Cup. It rates 48 teams, predicts every fixture, and simulates the tournament 10,000 times daily. Predictions are frozen before kickoff and graded publicly. The design goal is to make rigorous statistics feel like match-night atmosphere, not academic output. It's also Ian Mwendwa's portfolio flagship: engineering depth on display.

## Brand Personality

Technical, atmospheric, confident. The voice of a brilliant analyst who watches every match from a dark room with three monitors. Shows its work. States probabilities without hedging. Carries weight.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboard (blue sidebar, card-grid KPIs, "Upgrade to Pro" banners)
- Crypto/NFT dark UI (neon-glow abuse, particle effects, futurist tropes that shout "AI made this")

## Design Principles

1. **The data is the hero.** Typography and spacing serve the numbers, not the other way around. If a chart needs decorative framing to look interesting, the chart is wrong.
2. **Earn every element.** Nothing appears without earning its place. No intros that repeat the heading, no icons that merely restate the label.
3. **Match-night atmosphere, not broadcast noise.** Dark, considered, electric in restraint — not loud like a sports channel. The quiet between the noise.
4. **Confidence without theatre.** State predictions plainly. No hedging copy, no asterisked disclaimers buried in the UI. The model page handles caveats; match cards don't.
5. **Consistent visual weight.** Same conceptual importance gets same visual weight across all pages. A primary action on one page is a primary action everywhere.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Colour meaning always reinforced by shape or text (never colour alone for probability). Keyboard navigation must work through match cards.
